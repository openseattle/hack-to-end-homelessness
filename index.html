Hack to End Homelessness brings together Seattle's housing advocacy community and service providers with the best minds in technology and innovation. Our goal is to design and build solutions to all forms of homelessness, from the homelessness we see on the streets to the hidden problem of family homelessness.

Ending homelessness requires all kinds of hackers — developers, designers, statisticians, storytellers, marketers, and PR & fundraising pros! While we are selling tickets to help underwrite the costs of the event, no one will be turned away for lack of ability to pay. If you or someone you know needs financial assistance, please write to us at contact@hacktoendhomelessness.com.

Events:

Between May 1 and 4, 2014, we will host a series of events at Pioneer Square's Impact Hub:

  • Thursday, May 1: First Thursday ArtWalk in Pioneer Square with featured art at Impact Hub.
  • Friday, May 2, 7:00 pm: Seattle premiere screening of @home, featuring post-film discussion and reception with director Susanne Suffredin, Mark Horvath of InvisiblePeople.tv and Mark Putnam of the Committee to End Homelessness in King County.
  • Saturday and Sunday, May 3-4: Hack to End Homelessness, culminating in capstone presentations and a reception on Sunday at 5:00 pm.
